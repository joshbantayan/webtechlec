HTML PREPROCESSORS

What are HTML PREPROCESSORS?

Preprocessors are programs that convert a type of data to another type of data. A lot of HTML data and content may require you to enter the repeated data time and time again, and this may prove to be time consuming and inefficient. As such, preprocessors exist to ease the developing of HTML documents, where many contain redundant information that in turn will have to be managed.

As to basics, HTML preprocessors are classified into 3 classes:

1. Preprocessors with a syntax that clashes with HTML 2. Preprocessors with a syntax compatible with Standard Generalized Markup Language 3. Preprocessors with a syntax orthogonal to SGML or HTML

HTML Preprocessors also have the following different functionalities, of which are provided by various preprocessors:

1. Simple macros and no parameters
2. Macros with parameters
3. Document classesand inheritance
4. On-the-fly new tag definitions
5. Variable manipulation
6. Conditional codes
